Scotland short-term let licensing basics

Updated: 23 June 2026

Scotland’s short-term let licensing regime is stricter than much of England’s current setup. If you host in Scotland — even one property — licensing and safety evidence are usually on your radar alongside the certificates you already track for gas, electric and fire.

What hosts typically need to track

  • Short-term let licence — application, renewal date and licence number per property
  • Gas safety (CP12) — annual where gas appliances are present
  • EICR — fixed wiring; typically every five years
  • Fire risk assessment — reviewed regularly; linked to alarms and escape routes
  • Smoke / heat / CO alarms — installed and tested per Scottish guidance
  • Public liability insurance — STL-appropriate cover, renewed annually

Exact requirements depend on property type, council area and licence conditions. Check mygov.scot short-term lets and your local authority — this page is an overview only.

Why licensing trips hosts up

  • Licence expiry ≠ cert expiry — two different renewal cycles on two different portals
  • Condition changes — new hot tub, loft conversion or change of use can affect both licence and safety evidence
  • Multi-property hosts — each property may sit in a different council with different processing times
  • Platform listings — Airbnb/Booking may ask for licence numbers separately from safety uploads

Practical tracking system

Whether you use a spreadsheet or software, include a row for licence number + renewal alongside gas, EICR, FRA and insurance. Set reminders for both licence renewal and certificate expiry — missing either can stop trading or create platform problems.
