Airbnb fire safety uploads for UK hosts (2025)

Updated: 23 June 2026

Many self-managing UK hosts got caught out in 2025 when platforms tightened fire-safety questions and document uploads. This is separate from your annual gas safety (CP12) or five-yearly EICR — but guests, insurers and platforms increasingly expect you to prove you have both.

What platforms typically ask for

  • Confirmation you have a fire risk assessment (reviewed regularly)
  • Evidence of smoke and carbon monoxide alarms where required
  • Sometimes upload of fire-safety certificates or photos of alarm placement
  • Answers about escape routes, extinguishers and emergency info for guests

Exact requirements change by platform and listing type. Always read the current Airbnb or Booking.com host compliance centre — this guide is a checklist mindset, not legal advice.

Why uploads catch hosts out

  • Expiry vs issue date — a gas cert issued last month may not cover the upload window the platform shows
  • Multiple properties — each listing has its own upload screen; spreadsheets do not nudge you
  • PDFs in email — the certificate exists but you cannot find it when the app asks at 11pm
  • FRA not dated — fire risk assessments need review dates, not just “we did one in 2022”

Practical system (works with or without software)

  1. One folder per property: Gas, EICR, FRA, EPC, insurance, platform screenshots
  2. Calendar reminders at 30 / 14 / 7 days before expiry — not just annual anniversaries
  3. Monthly 10-minute review: open each property, check platform dashboard + cert dates
  4. Keep a guest-facing emergency sheet (exits, assembly point, utility shutoffs)
